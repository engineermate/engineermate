自2007年12月以来，建筑开支指数（Architecture Billings） 上升到最高水平

The December 2010 Architecture Billings Index score was 54.2, up from 52 the previous month, according to the American Institute of Architects. This score reflects an increase in demand for design services (any score above 50 indicates an increase in billings). The new projects inquiry index was 62.6, up slightly from a mark of 61.4 in November. Due to an approximate nine- to 12-month lag time between architecture billings and non-residential construction spending, this indicator suggests that the drag from commercial real estate investment will end by third-quarter 2011.

发现保护设备受水锤破坏的新方法

MIT researchers have uncovered a new process relating to the way drops of water spread after striking a surface, which could help engineers design more durable condensing surfaces, which are used in desalination plants and steam-based power plants. The research shows that the dynamics of moving droplets hitting a surface are quite different from droplets formed in place, specifically that such droplets undergo a rapid internal deceleration that produces strong pressures―a small-scale version of the water hammer effect.

水能够防止中东的骚乱吗？

The supply of potable water poses challenges for how Middle Eastern governments function and manage relationships with citizens, according to “Clear Gold: Water as a Strategic Resource in the Middle East.” Over the years, some Middle Eastern governments have provided cheap or even free water to ensure the consent of the governed, but when such programs were cut, instability often followed. Preventing future crises will depend on continuing to ensure adequate water supplies through investment in advanced technologies for water production, treatment, and reuse, as well as imposing comprehensive water pricing systems and offering incentives for responsible use.

IAPMO 修改吹净气体规范

The IAPMO Standards Council has issued Tentative Interim Amendments affecting the 2006, 2009, and 2012 editions of the Uniform Plumbing Code and the Uniform Mechanical Code. These TIAs address the hazards involved in the common industry practice of indoor gas purging at industrial, commercial and public facilities by requiring the discharge of purged gases to be directed outdoors and identifying specific requirements that must be met during the purging process.
