2012美国要减少联邦水经费

The House Appropriations Committee recently released its proposed FY2012 Interior and Environment Appropriations bill, which includes $7.1 billion in funding for the U.S. EPA, which is $1.5 billion less than last year’s level and $1.8 billion below the President’s request. Specifically, $967 million would be cut from Clean Water and Drinking Water State Revolving Funds. （阅读原文）

美国哪一个城市最绿？

San Francisco is, according to the U.S. and Canada Green City Index, a project that assessed 27 major U.S. and Canadian cities on environmental performance and policies based on carbon dioxide emissions, energy, land use, buildings, transport, water, waste, air quality, and environmental governance. New York, Seattle, Denver, and Boston round out the top five U.S. cities. （阅读原文）

乔治亚州在历时漫长的争水上诉中获胜

A federal appeals panel recently ruled in favor of Georgia in its legal battle with Alabama and Florida over usage of water from Lake Lanier by tossing out a lower court ruling that would have severely restricted the amount of water Georgia could withdraw starting next summer. The court also ruled, once and for all, that Georgia has a right to use water from the lake, but how much water the state will get is up to the Army Corps of Engineers. （阅读原文）

Spokane市获得雨水项目革新奖

The City of Spokane, Washington received the PISCES Award from the U.S. EPA for its SURGE (Spokane Urban Runoff Greenways Ecosystem) project, which utilizes urban storm garden boxes to capture runoff as well as porous surfaces to allow storm water to naturally absorb into the ground. （阅读原文）
