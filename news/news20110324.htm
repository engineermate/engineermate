明天是三角内衣工厂火灾100周年

On March 25, 1911, a fire in the Triangle Waist Co. garment factory in New York City killed 146 workers, including more than 60 people who died when they jumped from the building’s upper floors because their escape was blocked by locked doors. The deadliest accidental industrial fire in U.S. history, Triangle helped spark numerous reforms in American workplace safety measures.
