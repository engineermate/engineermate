Many pool drain covers meant to protect users from being trapped underwater by hundreds of pounds of suction are being used across the country even though the products underwent flawed safety evaluations, according to a Chicago Tribune investigation. The problem has occurred due to incorrect testing procedures that didn't accurately mimic the entrapment forces in a real pool or spa.

住宅消防喷淋系统能节水吗？

Residential sprinklers can be more efficient than extinguishing a fire via normal firefighting operations, says a new Fire Protection Research Foundation report. The amount of water used in fighting fires in homes without fire sprinkler systems can be more than 12 times higher than the amount discharged by a fire sprinkler system with a 10-minute operation.

In the Middle East, a water crisis caused by serious groundwater depletion may be an opportunity for regional peace, according to "The Blue Peace: Rethinking Middle East Water.” With input from local experts, the report recommends cooperative efforts among Turkey, Syria, Jordan, Lebanon, and Iraq to implement new technologies and water-monitoring programs to better manage the region’s water resources.

新法律将有助于保护关岛水资源

The U.S. EPA recently announced a decision to require all dischargers of urban storm water in Guam to obtain permits to control pollution in runoff from urbanized areas of the island. With this action, all current and future municipal storm water sources in Guam would follow Clean Water Act permitting requirements to minimize pollution into existing storm drain systems throughout the island.

John Swaffield was Emeritus Professor and former head of the School of the Built Environment and its Drainage Research Group at Heriot Watt University, Edinburgh. From 1996 to 2003, he was chair of the Water Regulations Advisory Committee of the UK government’s Department for Environment, Food, and Rural Affairs, and he was president of the Chartered Institution of Building Services Engineers for 2008-09.
