低流量大便器使得旧金山的污水系统散发臭气

使用低流量的大便器使得旧金山市的污水管道积聚更多的污泥，导致室内某些地方散发 臭鸡蛋 的气味。为了消除这种气味，市方购买了1，400万美元的漂白剂来消毒该市的（污）水。

宾夕法尼亚州要撤销住宅必须安装消防喷淋系统的法律

On Monday, the Pennsylvania House of Representatives voted 154-39 to repeal a law that went into effect on Jan. 1 that requires new home construction to include an automatic fire sprinkler system. The measure now heads to the state Senate for consideration.

美国人非常关心水质问题

More than one-half of Americans have concerns about the quality of the tap water in their homes, and only 45 percent are confident that their water source poses no health risk, according to a new survey commissioned by the Water Quality Association.

在经济衰退期间，持有博士学位的工程师的失业率要低于全国的平均水平

In October 2008, after the start of the recent economic recession, the increase in unemployment was less apparent among individuals with science, engineering, or health (SEH) doctorates, according to a recent National Science Foundation report. The unemployment rate was at 1.7 percent for SEH doctorate recipients compared with 6.6 percent for the general population.

加利福尼州立法收集雨水

加利福尼州 2011雨水收集法将允许土地所有者安装、维护和运行雨水桶和雨水收集系统来作某些指定的用途。

The Natural Resources Defense Council, Sierra Club, and Prairie Rivers Network have filed formal notice of intent to sue the Chicago Metropolitan Water Reclamation District for alleged violations of the Clean Water Act. The groups claim that the Chicago area is the largest single source of the phosphorus and nitrogen that feed life-killing algae in the Gulf of Mexico.

软水可以将衣服洗得更清洁

Using softer water in laundry machines is better at removing stains from clothes than increased water temperature or more detergent being used, according to a report by the Water Quality Association. The report also states that soft water leads to better appliance and fixture efficiencies versus hard water.

Due to strict EPA rules to combat cryptosporidium（隐孢子虫）, the Portland (Oregon) Water Bureau is planning on spending as much as $500 million to build a new ultraviolet treatment plant as well as replace the city's five open in-town drinking water reservoirs―even though two decades of monitoring have shown no evidence of a single case of drinking water-related cryptosporidiosis in the Portland area.
